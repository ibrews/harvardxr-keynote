10 Lessons
from 10 Years

Running an XR Enterprise Studio

Alex Coulombe
CEO, Agile Lens
Closing Keynote
Gund Hall, Harvard
2016
2016· Lesson 01 / 10

VR Is Theater.
Not Film. Not Games.

Everything changed when we stopped borrowing from cinema and started borrowing from live performance. Presence, liveness, co-authorship with the audience — those are theatrical ideas. They're the ones that actually work in VR.

Christmas Carol VR · Stage Presence · RSC
2017
2017· Lesson 02 / 10

The Best XR Teams
Look Nothing Like Tech Teams

Our biggest creative breakthroughs came when theater directors, lighting designers, architects, and motion capture artists were in the same room as engineers. XR is too new to have native experts — so we build them from everywhere.

Fisher Dachs Associates · Fisher Marantz Stone
2018
2018· Lesson 03 / 10

Know Which Work Pays
the Bills. Do Both.

Real estate visualization pays our salaries. VR theater and mixed reality art installations make us worth hiring. If you want to do ambitious, low-margin work — build a cash cow first. You need both, or eventually you lose both.

Diriyah Gate · Four Seasons · City of Apparition · Salle d'Or
2019
2019· Lesson 04 / 10

Being Sought-After
Is a Blessing and a Curse

Disney, the Kennedy Center, and the RSC all found us — we never pitched them. That's incredible. It also means we've never had to learn how to sell. Being sought-after and being able to pitch are different skills. Only one of them compounds.

2020
2020· Lesson 05 / 10

Require a Deposit.
Before You Write a Line of Code.

The single best operational decision we ever made. It filters out clients who aren't serious, protects the team from scope creep, and means our R&D never happens on our own dime. The first check separates the curious from the committed.

2021
2021· Lesson 06 / 10

Protect Time for
"We're Not Sure Either"

Our most common brief: "I don't know if this is possible, but…" The right answer is: "Honestly, us neither — let's find out together." That only works if Phase 1 of every project is explicitly dedicated to R&D. Build the discovery budget into the contract.

AI Dickens · Multi-Avatar (SIGGRAPH) · Space Opera · Salle d'Or
2022
2022· Lesson 07 / 10

Your Tool Choices
Are Business Strategy

Going all-in on Unreal Engine — including Gold Instructor certification — wasn't a technical decision. It was a five-year bet on which skills would still be valuable. Every major pipeline choice has a long tail. Treat it like a business decision from day one.

2023
2023· Lesson 08 / 10

Spatial Design Is
Its Own Discipline

It's not UX with depth. The best spatial thinkers we've hired came from theater, not software — because they already understood blocking, sightlines, presence, and audience agency. Stop borrowing screen-design patterns that don't fit. Hire for spatial thinking directly.

2024
2024· Lesson 09 / 10

Every Three Years,
Everything Changes. Ship Anyway.

DK1. Gear VR. Rift. Quest. Vision Pro. The platform reshuffles every few years and the "best" device rarely wins — the one people actually shipped on does. Don't wait for perfect. Build for now and design with the future in mind.

2025
2025· Lesson 10 / 10

We Were Always
Building for This Moment

Ten years ago, telling clients that spatial computing would reshape how they design buildings and stage shows felt like a hard sell. Today it's table stakes. Every uncertain, underpaid, "is this even possible?" project laid the foundation. We're just getting started.

2026
2026· BONUS LESSON

The Demo Will Break.
Budget for It.

In ten years — at Harvard, at SIGGRAPH, at the Kennedy Center — something has gone wrong every single time. A headset dies. The Wi-Fi drops. A character's head detaches from their body mid-show. The audience doesn't remember any of it. They only remember how the moment made them feel.
